Basquiat's chronology

  • 1960 - Born at Brooklyn Hospital, New York
  • 1964 - Starts displaying interest in drawing
  • 1967 - Basquiat attends a private catholic school. Creates a children's book, becomes a good athlete
  • 1976 - Transfers to the City-as-School designed for gifted and talented students. Meets Al Diaz who becomes his early artistic collaborator
  • 1977 - Involves with a drama group called Family Life Theater. Creates a fictional character called SAMO who makes a living selling fake religion. Begins spray-painting witty philosophical poems on the D train and around lower Manhattan
  • 1978 - Basquiat begins selling hand-made postcards to make a living. Andy Warhol was one of the first buyers
  • 1979 - An article about SAMO appears in the Village Voice drawing attention to his work
  • 1980 - Basquiat's art is publicly displayed for the first time in the Times Square Show
  • 1982 - His first one-artist exhibition at the Aninna Nosei Gallery
  • 1983 - Basquiat develops a friendly relationship with Andy Warhol
  • 1984 - Basquiat leaves for Hawaii where he rents a ranch and is often visited by friends
  • 1985 - His friends become concerned about his drug use. He is often found in a state of paranoia which is fueled by the threat of people stealing work from his apartment
  • 1988 - Basquiat dies on August 12 from a drug overdose

"Basquiat's great strength is his ability to merge his absorption of imagery from the streets, the newspapers, and TV with the spiritualism of his Haitian heritage, injecting both into a marvelously intuitive understanding of the language of modern painting" (Jeffrey Deitch).
